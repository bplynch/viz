Counties
Every four years election, people line up at the poll booths in counties across the United States to cast their vote for president.
Counties Sized by Votes
Some of these counties have millions of residents that vote, others have only hundreds.
Party Lines
In the U.S's two-party system, almost all candidates are elected from either the Democrat Party (blue) or Republican Party (red). In the last four elections, an third-party candidate has never carried a county in the Presidential election.
The Electorate
Democratic counties tend to be larger but fewer than Republican counties; Seen here, votes for Democratic candidates are concentrated in larger counties, whereas votes for Republicans are spread out across smaller counties.
Swing Counties
Some counties are won by the slimmest of margins. Those colored purple are tight races (within 2%) that may could be swung next time around.
